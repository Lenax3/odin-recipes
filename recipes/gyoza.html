Gyoza

Description

Gyoza are classic Japanese pan-fried dumplings filled with savory minced meat, fresh vegetables, and flavorful spices. They are crisp on the bottom, tender on top, and perfect for a delicious snack or dinner.

This recipe combines traditional flavors with fresh ingredients to create a juicy and authentic meal everyone will enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 30 gyoza wrappers
  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 2 cups napa cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp sake or Chinese rice wine

Steps

  1. Finely chop the cabbage, salt it, let it sit for 10 minutes, and squeeze out the excess water.
  2. In a bowl, thoroughly mix the ground pork, cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil.
  3. Place a wrapper on your hand, add a teaspoon of filling, wet the edge with water, and fold into pleats to seal.
  4. Heat oil in a pan, place the gyoza flat-side down, and fry for 2 minutes until the bottoms are golden-brown.
  5. Pour in a splash of water, quickly cover with a lid, and steam on medium heat for about 5 minutes.
  6. Remove the lid and let the remaining water evaporate until the bottoms become crispy again.
