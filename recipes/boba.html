Milk Bubble Tea with Tapioka

Description

Bubble Tea is a delicious and iconic Asian beverage famous for its signature layer of soft, chewy tapioca pearls at the bottom. Served ice-cold, it offers a delightful mix of creamy milk tea and sweet, textured boba bites.

This recipe guides you through cooking the perfect "Q-texture" tapioca pearls and mixing them with premium tea for a true cafe-style experience at home.

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil, gently drop in the tapioca pearls, and stir immediately to prevent them from sticking to the bottom.
  2. Cook the pearls uncovered on medium heat for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they float completely to the surface.
  3. Turn off the heat, cover the pot tightly with a lid, and let the pearls steam undisturbed for 20 minutes to soften the hard core.
  4. Drain the pearls through a colander and rinse them thoroughly under cold running water to stop the cooking process and remove excess starch.
  5. Transfer the cooled pearls to a small bowl, mix them with the brown sugar, and let them macerate for 10 minutes until a glossy, thick syrup forms.
  6. While the pearls rest, steep the black tea leaves in freshly boiled water for 5 to 7 minutes, strain out the leaves, and allow the liquid to cool.
  7. Spoon the syrupy tapioca pearls into the bottom of a tall glass, swirl them around the inner walls for a marbled look, then add ice, the cooled tea, and milk.
