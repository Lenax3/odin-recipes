Mochi

Description

Mochi is a traditional Japanese rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, known for its chewy texture and subtle sweetness.

This recipe shows how to make soft mochi at home.

Ingredients

  • 200 g glutinous rice flour (mochiko)
  • 180 ml water
  • 60 g granulated sugar
  • 50 g cornstarch or potato starch (for dusting)
  • 150 g sweet red bean paste (anko), optional filling

Steps

  1. Mix the glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water in a microwave-safe bowl until smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl loosely and microwave for 1 minute. Stir well, then microwave for another 1 minute. Stir again and microwave for a final 30–60 seconds until the dough is thick and translucent.
  3. Generously dust a clean surface with cornstarch or potato starch and transfer the hot mochi dough onto it.
  4. Let the dough cool slightly, then dust the top with more starch and flatten it to about 1 cm (1/2 inch) thick.
  5. Cut the dough into equal circles using a round cutter or glass.
  6. Place a small spoonful of sweet red bean paste (anko) in the center of each circle, then pinch the edges together to completely seal the filling.
  7. Roll each mochi gently in the starch to prevent sticking and brush off any excess before serving.
